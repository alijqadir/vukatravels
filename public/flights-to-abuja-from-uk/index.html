Frequently asked questions

What if I have checked baggage and want to book a low-cost fare from UK to Abuja?

Our low-cost fares don't include checked baggage. You can add baggage to your booking at an additional cost, or consider booking a higher fare family that includes baggage allowance.

Can I change my flight connection time when booking flights from the UK to Abuja?

You can request a change to your connection time during the booking process. However, please note that this may incur an additional fee and is subject to availability.

Do all flights from the UK to Abuja include free checked baggage allowance?

No, not all fares on our website include a checked baggage allowance. Check the fare details carefully before booking to see if your chosen fare includes baggage.

Can I book connecting flights from different UK airports to Abuja?

Yes, we offer connections from multiple UK airports to Abuja. Simply select your preferred departure airport during the booking process to get the best available options.

What are the baggage restrictions for infants and children on flights from the UK to Abuja?

For infants (under 2 years), one checked bag is included in the fare. For children (2-12 years), checked baggage allowance varies depending on the fare family booked.

How much does it cost to add extra baggage allowance to my booking from the UK to Abuja?

The cost of adding extra baggage allowance will depend on your chosen fare family and the airline operating your flight. This can be added to your booking during the checkout process.

About the destination

Abuja, the capital city of Nigeria, is a hub for business, culture, and politics. Located in the centre of the country, Abuja offers a unique blend of traditional and modern architecture. If you're planning flights to Abuja from the UK, here are some practical tips to keep in mind:

Baggage allowance: Check with your airline for their baggage policies, as they may vary. Most airlines allow 1 checked bag and 1 carry-on.

Connection times: Abuja's Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport (ABV) is a major hub with connections to many African cities. Plan ahead to ensure you have enough time between flights.

Flights from UK airports: Flights to Abuja depart from London, Manchester, and other major UK airports, often operated by airlines such as British Airways, Lufthansa, or Turkish Airlines.

Airport transfers: From the airport, taxis and car rentals are readily available. Be mindful of traffic during peak hours.

Local currency: The Nigerian Naira is the local currency; you can exchange it at the airport or withdraw from an ATM using a debit/credit card. Check with your airline for their recommended documents to carry on board.
