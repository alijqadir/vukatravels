Frequently asked questions

Can I book a flight to Dar es Salaam with a layover in another African city?

Yes, many airlines offer connecting flights through cities like Nairobi or Addis Ababa. This can sometimes be a cheaper option, but check the total travel time and any potential visa requirements.

Do I need to buy any additional insurance for my trip to Dar es Salaam?

It's always recommended to purchase travel insurance that includes medical cover, as well as baggage and trip cancellation insurance. This can provide peace of mind in case anything goes wrong during your journey.

What is the best time of year to visit Dar es Salaam?

The climate in Dar es Salaam is generally warm and sunny throughout the year, but the coolest months are from June to August. This period also sees fewer tourists, making it a good time to visit if you prefer smaller crowds.

Can I use my UK credit card in Dar es Salaam?

Yes, most major hotels and restaurants accept major credit cards like Visa and Mastercard. However, some smaller businesses may only accept cash or local debit cards.

How do I get from the airport to my accommodation in Dar es Salaam?

The Julius Nyerere International Airport is about 16 km from the city centre, and there are several options for getting to your accommodation. You can take a taxi, use a ride-hailing app like Uber, or book a private transfer service.

What vaccinations do I need before travelling to Tanzania?

It's always best to consult with your doctor or a travel clinic to determine the specific vaccinations you'll need. Generally, visitors to Tanzania require vaccinations against yellow fever and hepatitis A.
