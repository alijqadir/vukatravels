Frequently asked questions

What baggage allowance do I have on flights to Monrovia from the UK?

Our standard fare includes a 20kg checked baggage allowance, but some of our fares offer up to 30kg for a small supplement. You can add extra baggage in advance or at check-in if needed.

Can I book flights with layovers on this route?

Yes, we offer connecting flights to Monrovia from the UK via several European hubs. Be aware that your total travel time will be longer and you may need to collect luggage before re-checking it for the second leg.

How do I book a refundable ticket on flights to Monrovia?

Refundable tickets are available in our 'Flexible' fare family, which also includes perks like free changes and cancellations. You can switch between different fares or adjust your travel dates up until departure.

What's the policy on making changes to my Monrovia flights from the UK?

Most of our fares allow for changes made online or at a self-service kiosk without incurring fees, as long as you're within 24 hours of booking or up to three days before departure. Be sure to review your fare's change rules.

Can I choose my seat on flights from the UK to Monrovia?

Seat selection is available when booking or up until online check-in opens, depending on the fare you've chosen. Some seats have extra legroom or priority boarding – these can be added as extras in advance.

Do I need to pay for check-in baggage on some fares?

Yes, some of our lower-priced tickets do not include a checked baggage allowance. If you have one of these fares and want to bring luggage, you can add it at checkout or when you get to the airport.

Quick booking notes

When searching for flights to Monrovia from the UK, keep in mind the following

  • Baggage allowances vary greatly across airlines and routes. Check with your chosen airline directly for their specific policies on hand luggage, checked baggage, and excess fees.
  • Many flights to Monrovia involve connections en route. Be aware that some airlines may not allow connecting passengers to board the second flight if they missed the first leg, so factor in ample time between connections.
  • When booking a return ticket, ensure you're purchasing from the same airline or with a carrier that offers a aimd connection for onward travel. Some airlines have reciprocal agreements that simplify changes but aren't always clearly stated upfront.
  • If your travel dates are flexible, consider flying out on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, or Saturdays when possible to take advantage of lower fares and less congested routes to Monrovia.
  • Always check with a flight agent for the most up-to-date information and to ensure you're getting the best route for your needs. They can assist in securing quotes from multiple airlines and offer expert advice on navigating flights to Liberia's capital.

Start with your priorities (time, stops, baggage)

When searching for flights to Monrovia from the UK, it's essential to define your non-negotiables. Consider the following factors to guide your search:

  • Time: How quickly do you need to arrive in Monrovia? If time is of the essence, look for direct or one-stop flights that offer a reasonable journey time.
  • Stops: Are you willing to take a longer route with multiple stops or do you prefer a non-stop flight?
  • Baggage allowance: Check the airline's baggage policy to ensure it meets your needs. Some airlines may charge extra for checked bags, while others include more generous allowances. As you weigh up these priorities, keep in mind that there are often trade-offs involved:
  • Convenience vs cost: Direct flights can be expensive, but they save time and hassle.
  • Connection risk: One-stop flights can be cheaper, but you'll need to factor in the possibility of flight delays or cancellations.
  • Baggage fees: If you have a lot of luggage, it's essential to check the airline's policy before booking. By understanding your priorities and being aware of these trade-offs, you'll be better equipped to find a suitable flight that meets your needs.

Direct vs 1-stop vs 2-stops: making the trade-off

When booking flights to Monrovia from the UK, you're faced with a key decision: direct, 1-stop, or 2-stops? Each option comes with its own set of benefits and drawbacks. **Pricing- A direct flight is often the most expensive option.

  • However, it can also be the quickest way to reach Monrovia, saving you time in transit.
  • One-stop flights can be cheaper but may involve a longer journey overall.
  • Two-stops are generally the cheapest option but come with increased connection risks. **Convenience- Direct flights offer the most convenience, as you only need to worry about checking in and boarding once.
  • 1-stop flights require some extra planning, as you'll need to collect your luggage and re-check it for the next leg of your journey.
  • Two-stops can be particularly tiresome, with a higher chance of delays or lost luggage. **Connection Risk- Direct flights minimize connection risk, as there's no possibility of missing a connecting flight.
  • 1-stop flights carry some risk, especially if you're not familiar with the airport layout or have limited time for connections.
  • Two-stops are the most high-risk option, with multiple chances for things to go wrong. Ultimately, the decision between direct, 1-stop, and 2-stop flights comes down to your priorities: cost, convenience, or connection risk.
