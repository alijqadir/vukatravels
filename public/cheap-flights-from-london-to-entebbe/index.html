Frequently asked questions

Can I bring extra baggage on a flight from London to Entebbe?

Yes, most airlines allow additional baggage but you'll need to check with your chosen airline for their specific policy. They may charge extra for checked or hold luggage.

Do flights from London to Entebbe often get cancelled due to connections?

While cancellations can happen, they're less common on direct flights. However, if you have a connecting flight with a long layover in an African hub like Nairobi or Kigali, it's possible for delays or cancellations to occur.

What are the different fare families I can book from London to Entebbe?

You'll usually find Economy, Premium Economy, Business and First Class options. The main difference lies in seat comfort, legroom, meal quality and checked baggage allowances, so choose according to your budget and preferences.

Can I change my flight from London to Entebbe if needed?

Some airlines allow date changes for a fee or no charge at all, depending on the fare type you booked. If your flights are non-refundable, be aware that any changes may incur additional costs.

What time should I arrive at Entebbe International Airport before my flight?

To avoid rush and ensure smooth check-in, it's a good idea to get to the airport around 2-3 hours prior to your scheduled departure. This allows enough time for security checks and any last-minute paperwork.

Can I book connecting flights from London to Entebbe with multiple airlines?

Yes, many online booking platforms allow you to mix-and-match different airlines for a single journey. However, be aware that changing planes can sometimes lead to longer overall travel times or added costs.

About the Destination

Entebbe, located on the shores of Lake Victoria, is a bustling town in Uganda with a rich history and culture. As a popular tourist destination, Entebbe offers a unique blend of urban amenities and natural attractions.

  • Must-visit places:
  • The Entebbe Botanical Gardens, a lush oasis with over 300 plant species
  • The Uganda Wildlife Education Centre, a sanctuary for rescued animals
  • The Entebbe Town Beach, perfect for water sports or relaxation
  • Local experiences:
  • Take a ferry ride on Lake Victoria to explore nearby islands and villages
  • Visit the local markets for traditional crafts, fruits, and spices

- Try authentic Ugandan cuisine at one of the many restaurants in town

Activities and Excursions

From adventure seekers to nature lovers, Entebbe offers something for everyone. Here are some popular activities and excursions:

  • Wildlife safaris: visit nearby national parks like Murchison Falls or Queen Elizabeth National Park
  • Water sports: kayak, paddleboard, or take a boat tour on Lake Victoria
  • Cultural experiences:

- Visit the local museums to learn about Uganda's history and culture

- Attend a traditional performance or festival for an immersive experience

When to Go

Entebbe has a tropical climate with two main seasons:

  • Dry season (December to February): mild temperatures, clear skies
  • Wet season (March to May): occasional rain showers, lush vegetation

Consider the time of year that suits your preferences and plans.
