Frequently asked questions

Do I need any specific vaccinations before travelling to Zanzibar from London?

The World Health Organisation recommends that all travellers to Tanzania (where Zanzibar is located) should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations, such as MMR and tetanus. However, it's always best to consult your GP or a travel clinic for the latest advice on vaccinations.

Can I book flights from London to Zanzibar during peak season?

Yes, you can still find affordable flights from London to Zanzibar during peak season (June to October), but be prepared for higher prices and potential flight delays due to increased demand.

How do I get to the airport in time for my early morning flight from London to Zanzibar?

You can book a taxi or use public transport, such as the Tube and bus, to reach Heathrow or Gatwick airports with plenty of time to spare. Consider leaving your accommodation at least 3 hours before your scheduled departure time.

Can I bring pets on flights from London to Zanzibar?

Unfortunately, many airlines do not allow pets in the cabin or as checked baggage on flights to Zanzibar due to regulations and safety concerns. However, some airlines may offer pet-friendly services, so it's best to check with your airline directly.

Do I need a visa to enter Tanzania from London?

Yes, British citizens need to obtain an e-visa before arriving in Tanzania. You can apply for the e-visa online and pay the required fee, which currently stands at £50.

Can I book one-way flights from London to Zanzibar?

While some airlines may allow one-way bookings on their website or through a travel agent, it's often more expensive than booking a return ticket. Be aware that you may also face difficulties when trying to rebook or change your flight if needed.
