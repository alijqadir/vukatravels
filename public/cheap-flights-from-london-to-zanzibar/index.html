Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book my flights from London to Zanzibar?

It's recommended to book at least 2-3 months in advance for cheaper prices. However, if you're flexible with your travel dates, consider booking during the off-season for even better deals.

Can I get a refund or change my flight date if needed?

Check your airline's policy on refunds and changes. Some airlines may charge fees for amendments, while others offer more flexible policies.

What's the baggage allowance like on flights from London to Zanzibar?

Baggage allowances vary depending on the airline and class of travel. Check with your airline before booking to avoid any issues.

Do I need a visa to fly from London to Zanzibar?

No, you don't need a visa to transit through European or African airports. However, check with your airline for specific requirements.

Can I book a direct flight from London to Zanzibar?

Unfortunately, there are no direct flights available from London to Zanzibar. However, we can help you find the best connections and airlines for your needs.

What's the best time of year to visit Zanzibar?

Zanzibar's tropical climate is warm all year round. The best time to visit is during the dry season (December to March) when temperatures are slightly cooler and there's less rainfall.

Getting to Zanzibar from London: A Practical Guide

Step 1: Choose Your Flight Dates

When planning your trip, consider flying during the UK school holidays or off-season (usually May to June or September to November) for cheaper flights. Prices vary depending on demand and airline sales.

To find the best deals, use our live fare checks to compare prices across airlines and book in advance.

Step 2: Select Your Flights

When searching for flights from London to Zanzibar, keep an eye out for the following:

• Non-stop flights for a shorter journey time (approximately 8-10 hours)

• Direct flights with convenient connections in Europe or Africa

• Airlines offering free baggage allowances or competitive prices

Consider factors such as flight schedules, departure and arrival airports, and travel class.

Step 3: Check Baggage Allowances

Make sure to check your airline's baggage policy before booking. Some airlines may charge extra for checked bags, while others offer generous allowances.

To avoid any issues, pack smart and consider the following:

• Wear your heaviest or bulkiest items on the plane (coat, boots, etc.)

• Use packing cubes to maximize space in your luggage

• Label your bags clearly with your name, address, and contact details

Step 4: Plan Your Connections

If you're not flying non-stop, research your connections carefully. Consider:

• Flight times and layovers (aim for less than 2 hours between flights)

• Airport transfers and transportation options (e.g., shuttle buses or taxis)

Plan ahead to avoid any potential delays or complications.

Step 5: Book with Confidence

Once you've found your ideal flight, request a live quote from us. Our expert team will help you book your ticket and provide any necessary documentation.

Remember to check-in online and print your boarding pass at least 24 hours before your flight to avoid queues at the airport.
