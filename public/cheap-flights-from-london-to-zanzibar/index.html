Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book my flights from London to Zanzibar?

We recommend booking at least 6 weeks prior to your travel dates for the best prices.

What is the cheapest time of year to fly from London to Zanzibar?

Prices vary depending on demand, but generally speaking, flying during off-peak seasons (April-May or September-November) tends to be cheaper.

Do I need a visa to enter Tanzania?

As a UK citizen, you do not require a visa for entry into Tanzania. However, ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay.

How much baggage can I bring on my flight from London to Zanzibar?

Check with your airline for specific baggage allowances and policies. Most airlines offer checked baggage ranges from 20kg to 40kg (44lbs to 88lbs).

Can I book flights directly to Zanzibar Airport (ZNZ) or should I fly into Dar es Salaam?

Both options are available, but flying into Dar es Salaam might be more convenient depending on your accommodation and travel plans.

What is the best airline for direct flights from London to Zanzibar?

Several airlines offer direct flights, including British Airways, Turkish Airlines, and Emirates. Compare their prices, baggage policies, and in-flight amenities to choose the best option for you.

A Detailed Guide to Booking Cheap Flights from London to Zanzibar

If you're looking for a tropical getaway from the UK, Zanzibar is an excellent choice. Located off the coast of Tanzania, this island paradise offers stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a rich cultural heritage.

Step 1: Choose Your Travel Dates

The best time to visit Zanzibar is during the dry season, which runs from March to May or September to November. These periods offer pleasant weather, with average highs ranging from 25°C to 30°C (77°F to 86°F). Avoid visiting during the peak tourist season (June to August) if you prefer smaller crowds.

Consider the following factors when choosing your travel dates:

• Weather: Zanzibar's dry season offers ideal weather conditions for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

• Events and festivals: Research local events, such as the Zanzibar International Film Festival or the Stone Town Festival, which may interest you.

• Tourist season: Peak tourist season (June to August) brings larger crowds, while off-peak seasons offer more peaceful experiences.

Step 2: Research and Compare Flights

Use our live fare checks to compare prices across various airlines. Be sure to factor in additional costs, such as baggage fees and connection charges, when comparing quotes. Some popular airlines operating direct flights from London to Zanzibar include British Airways, Turkish Airlines, and Emirates.

When researching flights, consider the following:

• Airline reputation: Choose reputable airlines with good safety records and comfortable amenities.

• Flight duration: Opt for shorter flight durations or more convenient schedules if possible.

• Baggage policies: Check baggage allowances, fees, and restrictions to ensure they suit your needs.

Step 3: Check Baggage Allowances

If you plan to bring luggage or surfboards, ensure your chosen airline has a suitable baggage policy. Most airlines offer checked baggage allowances ranging from 20kg to 40kg (44lbs to 88lbs), depending on your ticket class.

Some popular airlines' baggage policies include:

• British Airways: 25kg checked baggage allowance for economy and premium economy classes.

• Turkish Airlines: 30kg checked baggage allowance for economy and business classes.

Step 4: Consider Layovers or Connections

If your flight involves a layover, research the airport's amenities, such as food options, Wi-Fi, and lounges. Some popular airports with comfortable amenities include:

• Dubai International Airport (DXB): Offers luxurious lounges, dining options, and entertainment.

• Istanbul Atatürk Airport (IST): Features comfortable lounges, shopping areas, and a range of dining options.

Step 5: Book Your Flight

Once you've found the best option for your travel dates, budget, and preferences, book your flight. Be sure to read and understand the airline's policies, including:

• Cancellation fees: Understand the charges for cancelling or changing your ticket.

• Refund policies: Know if you can get a full or partial refund in case of cancellations.

Tips for Booking Flights to Zanzibar from London

* Book in advance (at least 6 weeks prior) to secure the best prices.

* Consider flying during off-peak seasons for cheaper fares.
