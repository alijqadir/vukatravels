A detailed guide to booking flights to Kampala from the UK

If you're looking to book a flight to Kampala, Uganda's vibrant capital city, VUKA Travels can help. We specialise in live fare checks and provide expert guidance on baggage allowances and connections.

Step 1: Plan Your Trip

Before booking your flights, consider the time of year and weather conditions in Kampala.

* Dry season (December to February): Ideal for visiting the city's attractions.

* Peak travel periods: School holidays and major events like the Kampala International Film Festival can increase prices and crowds.

* Weather extremes: Be prepared for occasional rain showers or hot temperatures during other times of the year.

Step 2: Select Your Flight Type

Decide on the type of flight that suits your needs:

* Economy class: Budget-friendly option with standard amenities, but be aware that seats may be narrower and legroom more limited.

* Premium economy: Upgrade to more legroom and priority check-in for a moderate price increase.

* Business/first class: Enjoy luxurious comfort and gourmet meals, but expect significant price increases.

Step 3: Check Baggage Allowances

Make sure you understand the baggage allowances for your chosen flight:

* Hand luggage: Typically limited to 1-2 bags, depending on the airline and class. Check with your airline for specific requirements.

* Checked luggage: Varies by airline but usually includes a set weight limit (e.g., 20kg). Research your airline's policies to avoid surprises.

* Additional fees: Some airlines charge extra for excess baggage or special items like musical instruments, so factor these costs into your budget.

Step 4: Consider Flight Connections

If your flight has connections, ensure you have sufficient time between flights:

* Allow at least 2 hours for domestic connections and 3 hours for international ones. This allows time to clear customs, pick up baggage, and reach the next gate.

* Check the airport layout: Familiarise yourself with the airport's layout and transportation options to reach your next gate. Plan ahead to avoid delays or confusion.
