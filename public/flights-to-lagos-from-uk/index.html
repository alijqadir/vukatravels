Frequently asked questions

What are the baggage allowance rules for flights from the UK to Lagos?

Each airline has its own baggage policy, but as a general rule, most airlines allow one checked bag and one hand luggage per passenger. However, some budget airlines may charge extra for checked bags or have stricter weight limits. Be sure to check with your chosen airline for their specific baggage allowance rules before booking.

Can I book a flight from the UK to Lagos that includes a layover?

Yes, many flights from the UK to Lagos involve one or more connections in European cities such as Amsterdam or Paris. You can usually find these routes by searching for 'flights with layovers' or 'multi-city flights'. Keep an eye on connection times and consider booking a flight with a shorter layover if possible.

How do I know which fare family to book from the UK to Lagos?

Most airlines offer different fare families, such as economy, premium economy, business or first class. The best option for you will depend on your budget and travel preferences. If you're looking for a more comfortable flight experience, consider booking a higher fare family with additional perks like extra legroom or priority boarding.

What are the rules for changing my flight from the UK to Lagos?

If you need to make changes to your flight, check your ticket for any change fees or restrictions. Some airlines may charge a fee for altering your booking, while others may allow changes without penalty. Be sure to review your ticket conditions before making any changes to avoid additional costs.

Can I book a direct flight from the UK to Lagos?

Unfortunately, there are currently no direct flights available from the UK to Lagos. However, you can find routes with one or two connections that may be faster than booking separate flights. Use a route guide to compare options and find the best route for your needs.

Which airports in the UK offer the most convenient flights to Lagos?

London Heathrow is a major hub for many airlines operating flights to Lagos, while other popular departure points include Manchester and Birmingham. If you're flying from outside of these cities, consider using a regional airport with connections to London or another major hub.
