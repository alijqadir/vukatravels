About the destination

Nairobi, Kenya: A City of Contrast and Natural Beauty

Nairobi is a bustling metropolis in eastern Africa, offering an intriguing blend of urban sophistication and natural wonder. The city serves as a gateway to the vast wilderness areas surrounding it, making it an ideal starting point for safaris.

The climate in Nairobi is typically warm throughout the year, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F). The dry season, which usually runs from June to October, is often considered the best time to visit if you prefer clearer skies and more comfortable walking conditions. Conversely, the long rains that occur from March to May can make outdoor activities challenging.

Visitors often flock to Nairobi's vibrant markets, such as the Masai Market, where local handicrafts and souvenirs are sold alongside a variety of food options. The city is also home to several national museums, including the National Museum of Kenya, which showcases the country's history and culture.

Planning your days in Nairobi can be as laid back or action-packed as you choose. Popular activities include visits to the Giraffe Centre, where feeding these gentle creatures is a highlight for many, and the Karen Blixen Museum, which honors the life and work of the famous author.

While navigating the city's traffic can be overwhelming at times, Nairobi has made significant strides in recent years to improve its infrastructure. With the right guidance on connections and baggage arrangements, traveling from the UK couldn't be easier.
