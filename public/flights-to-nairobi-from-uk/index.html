About the destination

Nairobi, the vibrant capital city of Kenya, is a fusion of African culture and urban sophistication. As you step off your flight to Nairobi from UK, you'll be greeted by the warm sunshine and bustling streets.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Nairobi depends on your preferences. The dry season (June to October) is ideal for sightseeing, while the wet season (November to May) brings lush greenery and a more relaxed atmosphere. Temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C throughout the year.

What people do there

Nairobi is a city that seamlessly blends wildlife exploration with urban activities. Visitors often spend their days exploring the National Museum of Kenya, walking through the leafy Karura Forest, or visiting the Giraffe Centre and Elephant Orphanage. For some retail therapy, head to the Yaya Centre or Village Market.

Planning your days

To make the most of your trip, consider creating a daily itinerary that balances wildlife excursions with relaxation time. Nairobi's proximity to the Maasai Mara National Reserve makes it an ideal base for safari adventures. Typically, visitors spend two to three days exploring the city and surrounding areas before embarking on a longer safari expedition.

Getting around

Nairobi is relatively easy to navigate, with taxis and ride-hailing services widely available. If you prefer to drive, consider renting a car or booking a private transfer from the airport. Public transportation is also an affordable option for shorter journeys.

Accommodation and dining

From luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, Nairobi offers a diverse range of accommodation options. You'll find plenty of international restaurants serving cuisines like Indian, Chinese, and Italian, as well as traditional Kenyan dishes like nyama choma (roasted meat).
