Frequently asked questions

Can I bring my checked baggage allowance with me on connecting flights from the UK to Nairobi?

Yes, as long as you're flying with our airline and have an interlining agreement in place, your checked baggage allowance should be transferred seamlessly. However, please check your booking details for any specific baggage restrictions or fees.

What's the difference between 'Light' and 'Flexi' fares when booking flights to Nairobi from the UK?

Our Light fare offers a basic ticket price with no extras, while Flexi allows you to add checked baggage, seat selection, and other benefits. If you need these extras, it's worth upgrading to a Flexi fare for more flexibility.

Can I change my flight to Nairobi from the UK if I've already booked?

Yes, but please be aware that changes may incur fees and affect your ticket price. We recommend checking our Change Policy on the website or consulting with a travel agent for guidance.

Which airports in Kenya do flights to Nairobi from the UK typically land at?

Our flights usually land at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO), which is the main international airport serving Nairobi. However, please check your flight itinerary for specific details on arrival and departure airports.

How can I ensure my baggage meets the checked baggage requirements for flights to Nairobi from the UK?

To avoid any issues at check-in or during transit, please familiarise yourself with our airline's checked baggage policy, including size and weight restrictions. You may also want to consider checking your luggage as excess if it exceeds these limits.

Can I book a return flight from Nairobi to the UK at the same time as my outbound booking?

Yes, you can easily book a return ticket with our airline when making your initial booking. This way, you'll have everything taken care of and won't need to worry about coordinating separate bookings.

About the destination

Nairobi is a vibrant city with a mix of urban and natural attractions. It's home to many national parks, game reserves, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Some popular activities include:

  • Visiting the Nairobi National Park for a glimpse into African wildlife
  • Exploring the Giraffe Centre for an up-close encounter with these amazing creatures
  • Taking a walk in the Arboretum, a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city

Nairobi has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, there's something for everyone.

Some popular areas to stay include:

  • The city centre, close to shopping and entertainment
  • Westlands, known for its lively nightlife and restaurants
  • Upper Hill, with easy access to the city centre

Q: What are the best times to visit Nairobi?

A: Nairobi has a tropical climate with two rainy seasons. The dry season (June to October) is ideal for wildlife viewing.
