What is a connection?

A flight connection occurs when your initial flight lands in one airport and then you transfer to another flight departing from the same or a different airport to reach your final destination. Connections are commonly used by airlines to reduce direct flights between smaller routes, offering more frequent departures and better prices for passengers.

Benefits of connections

* Increased flexibility: With multiple departure times and airports, there's often a route that suits you best.

* Competitive pricing: Connecting flights can be cheaper than direct ones.

* Wider network: Connections give airlines the opportunity to offer routes between smaller or less frequent destinations.

However, consider the following potential downsides:

* Long layovers: This might result in extended travel times and fatigue.

* Baggage handling issues: If your checked luggage doesn't arrive with you on the connecting flight, it can be delayed or lost.

* Additional costs: Some airlines may charge for meals, baggage, or seat upgrades during connections.

Checklist for selecting a connection

Before booking a connection:

1. Check if the layover is long enough to accommodate customs and immigration procedures (typically at least 2-3 hours).

2. Verify that you can check your luggage through to Addis Ababa or if it will be held in London.

3. Research airline baggage policies, fees, and handling practices.

4. Ask about meal options during the layover; some airlines offer complimentary meals on connecting flights.

5. Consider seat upgrades for more comfort during long connections.

Questions to ask your airline

* What is my maximum connection time (MCT) before I risk missing my onward flight?

* Can I check-in online and print or download my boarding pass in advance, including the second leg of my journey?

* Do you offer any assistance for passengers with special requirements during connections?

About the destination

Addis Ababa: The Pearl of Africa

Located in the heart of Ethiopia, Addis Ababa is a vibrant city that blends traditional African culture with modern amenities. As one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Africa, it offers a unique blend of history and entertainment.

When to Visit

The best time to visit Addis Ababa is during the dry season from September to June, when temperatures are mild and rainfall is scarce. However, the city can be busy with tourists and locals alike during this period, so consider visiting in the shoulder season (April-May or September-October) for a more relaxed experience.

What to Do

Addis Ababa offers a wide range of activities for visitors:

* Explore the historic Merkato market, one of the largest markets in Africa

* Visit the National Museum of Ethiopia, which houses ancient artifacts and exhibits on Ethiopian history

* Take a stroll through the beautiful Entoto Hills, offering panoramic views of the city

* Enjoy traditional Ethiopian cuisine at one of the many local restaurants

Planning Your Days

With so much to see and do, planning your days is essential. Here are some tips:

* Start each day early to beat the heat and crowds

* Take a guided tour or rent a car to navigate the city's busy streets

* Be prepared for crowded markets and bazaars

* Don't miss trying traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremonies

Getting Around

While Addis Ababa is a relatively small city, traffic can be heavy. Consider using taxis or ride-sharing services to get around, and be mindful of the local road rules.

By following these tips and being open-minded about new experiences, you'll be well on your way to having an unforgettable trip to Addis Ababa!
