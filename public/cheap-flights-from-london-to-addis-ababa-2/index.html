Direct vs 1-stop vs 2-stops: choosing the right trade-off

When searching for cheap flights from London to Addis Ababa, you'll often come across options with varying numbers of stops. Understanding the pros and cons of each can help you make an informed decision.

Checklist for assessing direct, 1-stop, or 2-stop flights:

  • **Direct flights:**

- Pros:

* Less chance of delays due to connections

* More comfortable journey (no layovers)

* Easier baggage handling

- Cons:

* Potentially higher fares

- Limited availability

  • **1-stop flights:**

- Pros:

* Often cheaper than direct options

* Reduced travel time compared to 2-stops

- Cons:

* Risk of layover delays or missed connections

* More complex baggage handling (check with airlines)

  • **2-stop flights:**

- Pros:

* Can be even cheaper than 1-stops

* Wide availability and flexibility in schedules

- Cons:

* Longer total travel time

* Higher risk of layover delays or missed connections

Questions to ask:

* What is the total travel time, including layovers?

* Are there any significant changes in aircraft type during my journey?

* How will baggage be handled between flights? Will it need re-checking or tagging?

Trade-offs:

* Time vs Cost: If you're on a tight schedule, direct flights might not always be the best option due to higher fares. However, if you prioritize saving money, 1-stop or 2-stop options could work for your budget.

* Comfort vs Convenience: Direct flights offer more comfort and convenience but may come with a price premium.

When choosing between these trade-offs, consider your priorities: time, cost, comfort, or flexibility in travel schedules. By weighing the pros and cons of each option, you can make an informed decision that suits your needs and budget.

About the destination

Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Located in the heart of East Africa, Addis Ababa is a vibrant and eclectic city steeped in history and culture. As the capital of Ethiopia, it offers a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions that are sure to captivate visitors from around the world.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Addis Ababa depends on your preferences. The dry season (September to May) is ideal for sightseeing, while the rainy season (June to August) brings lush greenery and lower temperatures. Typically, the weather remains mild throughout the year, making it an excellent destination for visitors who want to escape harsh winters or scorching summers.

What to Do

Addis Ababa has plenty to offer:

* Explore the vibrant markets, such as the Mercato, which is one of the largest in Africa

* Visit the National Museum, home to Lucy, the famous 3.2 million-year-old fossil

* Take a stroll through the Addis Ababa City Hall's beautiful gardens and lake

* Learn about Ethiopia's rich history at the Red Terror Martyrs' Memorial Museum

* Sample local cuisine, such as injera, tibs, or misir wot

Planning Your Days

To make the most of your time in Addis Ababa:

* Allow 3-4 days to explore the city and its surroundings

* Prioritize must-see attractions, such as the National Museum and the Mercato

* Take a day trip to the nearby Ankober Mountains or Lake Langano for breathtaking views and outdoor activities

* Be prepared for variable traffic conditions and crowded streets

Baggage Guidance

When packing for Addis Ababa:

* Pack light, breathable clothing suitable for mild weather conditions

* Don't forget essentials like sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable walking shoes

* Consider the cultural significance of traditional dress in Ethiopia when selecting attire
