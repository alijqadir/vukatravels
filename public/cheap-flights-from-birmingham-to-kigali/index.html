About the destination: Kigali, Rwanda

Kigali is a vibrant and rapidly developing city in the heart of East Africa. Located at an altitude of over 1,400 meters, the city boasts pleasant year-round temperatures, with average highs ranging from 18 to 22°C (64-72°F). The best time to visit Kigali is during the dry season, typically from May to September or December to February.

When you land in Kigali, you'll be immersed in a unique blend of traditional and modern African culture. The city's compact size makes it easy to explore on foot or by taxi, and visitors often start their day with a visit to the Kigali Genocide Memorial Centre, a poignant reminder of Rwanda's history.

With a rich natural heritage, Kigali is an ideal base for exploring the surrounding countryside. Many visitors take guided tours to the nearby Volcanoes National Park, where they can trek to see mountain gorillas in their natural habitat. Other popular activities include visiting the Kibira Forest, hiking to the top of Mount Kigali, and exploring the city's many markets and craft shops.

Planning your days in Kigali is relatively straightforward. Most tourists stay in one of the city's many mid-range hotels or guesthouses, which offer a range of amenities including restaurants, bars, and tour services. The city has an efficient network of taxis and ride-hailing apps, making it easy to get around without a car.

While Kigali is generally a safe city for tourists, visitors are advised to exercise caution when using public transportation or walking alone at night. It's also worth noting that some areas of the city can be quite hilly, so comfortable shoes are essential for exploring the city on foot.

In terms of packing, light layers and waterproof clothing are recommended due to Kigali's high altitude and variable weather. Visitors should also bring sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses to protect themselves from the strong equatorial sun. With its unique culture, stunning natural scenery, and rich history, Kigali is an exciting and rewarding destination for travelers of all ages.
