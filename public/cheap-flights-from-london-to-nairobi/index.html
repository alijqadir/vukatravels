Frequently asked questions

Can I get compensation if my flight from London to Nairobi is delayed?

If your flight is delayed, you may be eligible for reimbursement under EU regulations. However, please note that these regulations only apply to flights departing from the UK until December 31st, and you should contact your airline directly to discuss possible compensation.

How can I ensure my baggage is protected during transit?

All airlines operating from London to Nairobi have adequate insurance coverage for lost or damaged baggage. However, it's always a good idea to check with your airline and review their baggage policies before booking your flight.

What are the chances of my luggage being checked through to Nairobi?

Your luggage will be checked through to Nairobi as standard practice for most airlines operating from London. However, this may not always be the case, so it's best to confirm with your airline directly.

Can I get a seat upgrade if I book my flight at short notice?

Seat upgrades are typically available on a first-come-first-served basis and may require an additional fee. However, some airlines may offer upgrade options at check-in or shortly before departure, so it's always worth checking with your airline.

What happens if I miss my connecting flight from London to Nairobi?

If you miss your connecting flight, your airline will usually rebook you on the next available flight. However, this may not be the same day or at a convenient time, so it's essential to check with your airline and plan accordingly.

Can I use my frequent flyer miles to book a seat from London to Nairobi?

Some airlines offer redemption options for flights from London to Nairobi using frequent flyer miles. However, these seats are typically limited, so it's best to check with your airline and plan ahead.

About the destination: Nairobi, Kenya

Nairobi, the vibrant capital of Kenya, is a melting pot of African culture and excitement. As you touch down at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, you'll be immersed in the city's infectious energy.

When to visit

The best time to visit Nairobi is during the dry season, from June to October or December to February. These periods offer pleasant weather, ideal for outdoor exploration and activities like safaris. Avoid visiting during the long rains (March to May) when roads can be slippery and muddy.

What to do

* Explore the bustling Gikomba Market, a hub of local commerce and culture

* Visit the David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage, where you can observe rescued baby elephants being rehabilitated

* Take a stroll through the Karura Forest, an oasis in the heart of the city with stunning views

Planning your days

* Nairobi is a 24-hour city, so don't be surprised to find shops and restaurants open late into the night

* Plan your itinerary according to your interests – wildlife enthusiasts will love visiting the nearby National Parks, while culture vultures can spend hours exploring museums and galleries

* Don't miss trying some of Kenya's delicious cuisine, including local dishes like nyama choma (roasted meat) and ugali (cornmeal porridge)

Baggage and connections

* Check with your airline for baggage restrictions and fees before flying from London to Nairobi

* Consider booking flights with a layover in Europe or the Middle East to break up the long journey, but be mindful of potential delays and connection times

With its unique blend of African charm and urban sophistication, Nairobi is an exciting destination that will leave you wanting more. By understanding the local culture and planning your trip thoughtfully, you'll get the most out of this incredible city.
