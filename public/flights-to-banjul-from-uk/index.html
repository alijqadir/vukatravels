Frequently asked questions

What are the baggage allowance rules for flights from UK to Banjul?

As a guide, most airlines operating this route offer 20-25 kg checked baggage allowance and 7-10 kg hand luggage allowance. However, please check your chosen airline's baggage policy when booking as this may vary.

What are my options for connections if I'm flying from UK to Banjul?

You can expect a layover in West Africa or Europe with most airlines operating flights to Banjul from the UK. Some carriers also offer non-stop flights, so it's best to check your booking confirmation for details.

Can I book different fare types (e.g., Economy, Premium) for my family when flying from the UK to Banjul?

Yes, most airlines operating this route allow you to book different fare types within a single booking. However, please note that each passenger's fare will be calculated individually based on their chosen ticket type.

What happens if I need to make changes to my flight from the UK to Banjul?

Some airlines operating this route may charge for changes made to bookings, while others might not. Check your booking confirmation or the airline's website for their change policy and any applicable fees.

Are there different airport options when flying from the UK to Banjul?

While many airlines operate direct flights from London or Manchester to Banjul, you can also fly with carriers that involve a layover in Europe or West Africa. Your chosen airline will determine which airport(s) are available for your specific booking.

Can I book a flight from the UK to Banjul on a budget-friendly fare and still have some flexibility if my travel plans change?

Some airlines offer more flexible fares with fewer restrictions, while others may charge extra for changes. Be sure to review your chosen airline's fare conditions before booking to ensure it meets your needs.

About the destination

Banjul, situated at the tip of The Gambia's Atlantic coast, is a bustling port city with rich history and culture. When planning your flights to Banjul from UK, consider the following essential information: When landing in Banjul, you'll likely arrive at Yundum International Airport (BJL). From there, take a taxi or shuttle bus into town. Note that traffic can be heavy during peak hours.

Baggage allowance: Check with your airline for specific baggage policies, as some may offer free checked bags or charge extra.

Flight connections: Be aware of potential layovers and choose airlines offering convenient schedules to minimize waiting times.

Weather: Banjul experiences a hot and dry climate year-round; pack accordingly.

Time difference: The Gambia is in the same time zone as Western Europe, making it relatively easy for UK travelers.

Currency: The Gambian dalasi (GMD) is the local currency, but you can exchange or withdraw cash at banks and ATMs.
