Direct vs 1-stop: how to choose

When planning your flights to Karachi from the UK, one of the key decisions you'll need to make is whether to take a direct flight or a route with a connection. Here's a quick guide to help you weigh up the pros and cons:

  • Price: If you're on a tight budget, a 1-stop flight might be your best option. Many airlines offer competitive prices for routes with connections in major hubs like Dubai or Istanbul.
  • Convenience: Direct flights can save you time and hassle by avoiding the need to collect and re-check your luggage at each stopover. However, this convenience comes at a cost - often significantly higher than a 1-stop option. When choosing between direct and indirect flights, it's essential to consider connection risk. Even with the best laid plans, delays can occur due to factors like weather or air traffic control issues.
  • Connection guidance: If you do opt for a 1-stop flight, make sure to check your airline's baggage policy carefully. Some carriers may charge extra fees for checked luggage on connecting flights.
  • Baggage and seat selection: Keep an eye out for any restrictions on luggage size or weight, as well as seat selection options when booking your tickets. It's worth noting that some airlines offer more generous baggage allowances than others, so be sure to check the specific policies of each airline before making a decision.

About the destination

Karachi is Pakistan's largest city, situated on the Arabian Sea coast. It offers a unique blend of modern and traditional culture. Plan your trip with our helpful guidance:

Karachi has two international airports: Jinnah International Airport (KHI) and Quaid-e-Azam International Airport.

Check-in baggage allowance varies by airline; some offer 23kg, while others allow up to 30kg per passenger.

Most UK flights to Karachi involve a connection in Middle Eastern cities like Dubai or Abu Dhabi.

Pakistani rupees can be exchanged for British pounds at airports or in city centres.

Make sure you have the correct power adapter and any necessary travel documents before your journey.

Getting around Karachi

Karachi's public transport system is extensive, with buses, taxis, and rickshaws available. You can also book private car services through local operators.
