Frequently asked questions

Can I bring my surfboard on a flight from UK to Accra?

Yes, most airlines allow sports equipment like surfboards in the hold, but you'll need to contact them in advance to book and pay for this service. Be prepared for extra charges and check with your airline about any specific requirements or restrictions.

What's the best way to get a cheap flight from London to Accra?

Consider flying into Kotoka International Airport (ACC) rather than a regional airport in Ghana, as this often offers more competitive fares. Additionally, being flexible with your travel dates and booking well in advance can also help you find cheaper flights.

Can I make changes to my flight itinerary if I'm flying from Manchester to Accra?

Most airlines allow some flexibility with changes, but this often depends on the fare family you've booked. If you've purchased a flexible ticket, you can usually make changes without penalty, whereas non-flexible tickets may incur charges.

How many bags am I allowed to check-in for my flight from Birmingham to Accra?

Typically, economy passengers are allowed one checked bag weighing up to 23kg, while business and first-class passengers usually get more generous baggage allowances. Always check with your airline for their specific policy on baggage.

Can I book a direct flight from UK to Accra?

Unfortunately, there are no direct flights available from the UK to Accra, so you'll need to consider flights with connections. Be sure to research and choose an airline that offers reasonable layover times and suitable routes.

What's the cheapest fare family for a flight from Glasgow to Accra?

Budget-conscious passengers might want to consider the 'Economy Light' or 'Basic Economy' options, which usually offer lower fares but with fewer perks. However, be aware that these tickets often come with restrictions on baggage and seat selection.
