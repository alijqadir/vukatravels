Direct vs 1-stop: what you gain and what you risk

If you're planning flights to Accra from the UK, you'll often have a choice between direct and one-stop options. While both can get you to your destination, they differ significantly in terms of price, convenience, and connection risks.

Price

Direct flights typically come with a higher price tag compared to one-stop options. This is because airlines factor in the costs associated with layovers when setting their prices for single-destination tickets. However, if you're short on time or value your comfort over cost savings, direct flights might be worth the splurge.

Convenience

One-stop flights often offer more flexibility and convenience. They can be a better option if you need to make changes to your itinerary or want to break up your travel into manageable chunks. With one-stop flights, you'll have the chance to stretch your legs, grab some food, or catch up on work during your layover.

Connection risk

One of the biggest downsides to one-stop flights is connection risk – the possibility that your connecting flight might be delayed or cancelled due to unforeseen circumstances. To mitigate this risk, look for airlines with a strong track record for reliability and consider booking seats near the front of the plane for easier boarding and disembarking.

  • Consider packing essentials like medication, chargers, and adapters in your hand luggage to minimize the impact of potential delays.
  • If you're connecting in a busy hub like Accra's Kotoka International Airport, plan to arrive early and account for extra time spent navigating security checks and finding your gate.
  • Choose an airline with a generous baggage allowance or consider paying for additional checked bags to avoid any last-minute stress.
  • Be mindful of peak travel seasons when demand is high and connection risks are more likely.
