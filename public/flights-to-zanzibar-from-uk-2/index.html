About the destination

Zanzibar, a tropical island paradise

Located off the coast of Tanzania in East Africa, Zanzibar is an archipelago of beautiful islands that offer a unique blend of African, Arab, and Portuguese influences. With its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant culture, Zanzibar has become a popular destination for UK travelers.

Best time to visit

Zanzibar's tropical climate means it's warm year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from June to October or December to March. These periods offer comfortable temperatures, low humidity, and minimal rainfall, making it ideal for outdoor activities like snorkeling, diving, or simply relaxing on the beach.

Things to do in Zanzibar

With its rich history and culture, there's plenty to explore on this enchanting island:

* Visit the historic Stone Town, with its narrow alleys, bustling markets, and picturesque architecture.

* Explore the beautiful beaches, such as Nungwi or Matemwe, where you can snorkel, dive, or simply soak up the sun.

* Take a traditional dhow boat tour to explore the surrounding islands and coral reefs.

* Experience the local culture by attending a traditional festival or trying some delicious seafood at one of the many local restaurants.

Planning your days

Zanzibar is relatively small, making it easy to get around without renting a car. You can use taxis, tuk-tuks, or even walk to explore the island. Typically, you'll want to spend 3-5 days in Zanzibar to experience all that it has to offer.

* Spend your first day exploring Stone Town and getting accustomed to local customs.

* Dedicate one day to water activities like snorkeling or diving.

* Take a boat tour or visit nearby islands on another day.

* Use the last day for relaxation, shopping, or visiting any attractions you may have missed earlier.

Baggage guidance

When packing for Zanzibar, remember that:

* The island has a tropical climate, so bring light, breathable clothing and comfortable shoes.

* Don't forget your sunscreen, sunglasses, and hat to protect yourself from the sun.

* If planning water activities, consider bringing a reef-safe sunscreen or purchasing one locally.

Connection guidance

Zanzibar is easily accessible by air, with many UK airlines operating direct flights. You can also connect through major African hubs like Dar es Salaam or Nairobi. Always check your flight connection options and book in advance to avoid delays or cancellations.

Remember to always check the latest travel advice from your airline before booking your trip.
