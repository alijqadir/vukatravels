About the destination

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Located in the northern part of the country, Islamabad is nestled in the foothills of the Margalla Hills and surrounded by lush green forests.

  • **Must-visit attractions**:

- Faisal Mosque: A stunning mosque that blends modern architecture with traditional Islamic design

- Pakistan Monument: A symbol of national unity and pride

- Rawal Lake: A beautiful lake surrounded by scenic hiking trails and picnic spots

  • **Experience the local culture**:

- Visit the Lahore Museum to learn about the rich history and art of Pakistan

- Explore the bustling markets, such as the Islamabad Bazaar, for a taste of local cuisine and shopping

- Attend a traditional Pakistani festival or event to experience the vibrant culture firsthand

Tips for a smooth journey

To ensure a hassle-free trip from London to Islamabad, consider the following:

  • **Check your passport**: Ensure it is valid for at least six months beyond your intended stay in Pakistan
  • **Pack wisely**: Bring layers for cooler mornings and evenings, as well as sunscreen and sun hats for daytime excursions
  • **Research local customs**: Respect local traditions and dress modestly when visiting mosques or other cultural sites

Get the most out of your trip

To make the most of your time in Islamabad, consider exploring the surrounding region. The Margalla Hills offer scenic hiking trails, while the nearby city of Rawalpindi is famous for its historical landmarks and vibrant markets.

  • **Visit the nearby cities**: Explore the history and culture of Rawalpindi, or visit the ancient city of Taxila
  • **Take a day trip**: Visit the beautiful lake at Khanpur, or explore the scenic valleys of the Margalla Hills
