Tips for First-Time Travellers to Islamabad

If you're planning your first trip to Pakistan, there are a few things to keep in mind to make the most of your journey.

Getting Around Islamabad

Islamabad is a relatively small city compared to other major cities in Pakistan. You can easily get around by taxi or ride-hailing services like Uber and Careem. We recommend downloading the relevant apps on your phone before arriving, as they are widely used in the country.

Must-Visit Attractions

Some of the top attractions in Islamabad include:

  • Faisal Mosque: A stunning mosque that is one of the largest in Pakistan
  • National Museum of Pakistan: A great place to learn about the history and culture of Pakistan
  • Shakarparian Park: A beautiful park with great views of the city
  • Lake View Park: A peaceful oasis in the heart of the city

When to Visit Islamabad

Islamabad is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. If you prefer cooler weather, October to February are ideal months, while March to September offer warmer temperatures.

About the Destination

History and Culture

Islamabad was built in the 1960s as the new capital of Pakistan. It is a city that seamlessly blends traditional and modern architecture. Visitors can experience the rich culture and history of Pakistan through its museums, monuments, and cultural events.

Language

Urdu is the official language of Pakistan, but English is widely spoken in Islamabad. You may also hear some local dialects like Punjabi or Pashto.

Currency

The Pakistani rupee (PKR) is the local currency. We recommend exchanging your money at a reputable exchange office or using an ATM to withdraw cash.
