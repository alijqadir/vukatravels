Frequently asked questions

What's included in my fare if I book a 'Basic' flight from Birmingham to Lagos?

With our 'Basic' fares, you'll get a seat on the plane and the opportunity to pay for extras like checked baggage and meals. If you'd like more flexibility with your booking or priority boarding, consider upgrading to one of our higher fare families.

Will I be able to check in my luggage for free if I book a 'Light' flight from Birmingham to Lagos?

No, with our 'Light' fares, you'll need to pay extra for checked baggage. This can be added when you make your booking or as an amendment later.

How do I avoid getting stuck in a long layover if I'm flying from Birmingham to Lagos?

We recommend selecting flights with the shortest connection times, usually around 60-90 minutes. This will give you time to clear customs and make your onward flight without feeling rushed.

Can I change my return date if I book a 'Return' fare from Birmingham to Lagos?

Yes, but be aware that doing so may incur an amendment fee. It's best to try and make changes during office hours when our team can assist you.

What airport in Lagos should I choose if I book my flights with FlightGuide?

Both Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS) and Ilorin International Airport (ILR) serve the city, but LOS is generally busier and more convenient for most travelers.

Will I be able to bring extra baggage if I book a 'Premium' flight from Birmingham to Lagos?

Yes, our 'Premium' fares often come with extra baggage allowance, but it's always best to check the details of your specific fare family when making your booking.

Direct vs 1-stop vs 2-stops: making the trade-off

When searching for cheap flights from Birmingham to Lagos, you'll often find a range of options. Understanding the differences between direct, one-stop, and two-stop flights can help you make an informed decision that suits your needs.

Price Considerations

  • Direct flights tend to be more expensive than their indirect counterparts.
  • One-stop flights may offer a cheaper option, but this depends on various factors such as airline, route, and layover duration.
  • Two-stop flights are usually the cheapest of all, but they come with increased connection risk (see below).

Convenience and Connection Risk

  • Direct flights eliminate the need for connections, making them more convenient for some travelers.
  • One-stop flights offer a balance between convenience and cost savings.
  • With two-stop flights, you'll have to factor in potential delays or lost luggage due to longer travel times.

Baggage and Layover Tips

  • If you're traveling with checked baggage, be aware that one-stop flights may involve a higher risk of delayed or lost luggage.
  • When booking a two-stop flight, make sure to check the layover duration to ensure it's reasonable for your schedule and connection requirements.

Connection buffers that keep your trip smooth

When searching for cheap flights from Birmingham to Lagos, it's essential to consider the connection buffers in your itinerary. A well-planned layover can make all the difference between a seamless travel experience and a stressful one. A buffer is an extra amount of time added to your journey to account for unexpected delays or changes in flight schedules. This can be particularly important when traveling from Birmingham to Lagos, where connections are often tighter due to longer flights and potential weather-related disruptions. Here are some tips for finding safer layovers and avoiding risky tight connections:

Always check the minimum connection times recommended by airlines, but also consider adding a buffer of at least 1-2 hours in case of delays.

Look for itineraries with fewer changes, as these tend to be less prone to disruptions.

Be wary of flights with very short layovers (less than 30 minutes) or those that involve multiple transfers.

Consider booking with airlines that have a good track record of on-time performance and minimal cancellations.

Check the airport's operational hours and any potential construction or maintenance-related disruptions before finalising your travel plans.
