About the destination: Islamabad, Pakistan

Islamabad is a city of lush greenery and modern architecture, nestled in the heart of the Margalla Hills. It's a great destination for those looking to experience the unique blend of traditional Pakistani culture and modern amenities.

When to Visit

The best time to visit Islamabad is during the spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. These periods offer the perfect opportunity to explore the city's scenic beauty, with temperatures ranging from 15-25°C (59-77°F). Winters can be cool, while summers are hot and humid.

Things to Do

Islamabad offers a wide range of activities for visitors, including:

* Exploring the stunning natural scenery, such as the Margalla Hills and Lake Saif ul Malook

* Visiting the Faisal Mosque, one of the largest mosques in the world

* Discovering the city's rich cultural heritage at the Pakistan National Museum

* Enjoying the scenic views from the top of the Daman-e-Koh viewpoint

Planning Your Days

With its moderate climate and varied attractions, Islamabad is an ideal destination for a short break or longer holiday. A typical day in Islamabad might begin with a visit to the Faisal Mosque or a trek in the Margalla Hills. Afternoon visits could include exploring the city's many museums or shopping at one of the local markets.
