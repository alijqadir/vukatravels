About the destination

Lagos, a vibrant and bustling city in Nigeria, offers an unforgettable experience for visitors. With its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture, Lagos is a melting pot of traditions and modernity.

Best time to visit

The dry season from November to March is considered the best time to visit Lagos, with comfortable temperatures and minimal rainfall. However, this period also sees increased tourist activity, so be prepared for larger crowds.

What to do in Lagos

* Explore the city's iconic landmarks such as the National Museum and the Tafawa Balewa Square

* Visit the bustling markets, like the Idumota Market, where you can find everything from local handicrafts to fresh produce

* Take a stroll along the picturesque Lekki Conservation Centre, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape

Planning your days

With so much to see and do in Lagos, it's essential to plan your itinerary carefully. Typically, visitors allocate 3-5 days for exploring the city, with at least one day dedicated to visiting nearby attractions like the Epe Hills or the Badagry Slave Route.

To make the most of your trip, consider booking a guided tour that can help you navigate the city's complexities and provide valuable insights into its history and culture. Alternatively, take advantage of Lagos' thriving public transportation system, which includes buses and taxis.

Getting around

Lagos is a sprawling metropolis, and getting from one place to another can be time-consuming. The airport, Murtala Mohammed International Airport (LOS), receives flights from various international destinations, including London. From the airport, you can take a taxi or shuttle service to your accommodation.

Tips for planning your trip

* Be prepared for crowded streets and chaotic traffic

* Haggle prices at markets and with vendors

* Try local cuisine, which often features fresh seafood and spicy dishes

* Respect local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting mosques or churches
